notes:
## Institution types
- DNP tables = Pengeinstitutter (banks only, not mortgage banks)
- DNR tables = Realkreditinstitutter (mortgage banks only)
- DNM tables = MFI-sektoren (banks + mortgage banks combined, excluding Nationalbanken)
- DNMUF tables = microdata loan statistics (loan-level distributions, from 2020)
- DN tables without P/M/R = Danmarks Nationalbank specifically (dnintval, dnbetal, dnvali, dnprnd, dnsnb1, dnsnb2, dnra)
- DNUD tables = udlånsundersøgelse (lending survey, quarterly credit conditions index)

## Which table for which question
- Total bank deposits (indlån): dnpindk (domestic, by sector/instrument/maturity, from 2003) or dnpin (incl. foreign counterparts, from 2003) or dnmin (MFI sector = banks+mortgage banks)
- Bank deposits by industry sector: dnpindkb (from 2013)
- Total bank lending (udlån from banks): dnpuddk (domestic, by sector/instrument/maturity, from 2013) or dnmud (MFI sector combined, from 2003)
- Bank lending by interest type or afdrag: dnpuddk2 (interest type + afdrag + rentefix), dnpuddk3 (interest type + instrument)
- Bank lending by industry sector: dnpuddkb (from 2013)
- Mortgage (realkreditlån) lending: dnruddkb (from 2014, by sector/currency/industry); dnruddk (more dimensions incl. rentetype/afdrag/ejendomskategori, but only from 2022)
- MFI sector full balance sheet: dnbala (aggregated line items) or dnbald (disaggregated by maturity/sector/currency within each line item), both from 2003
- Money supply (M1/M2/M3): dnmnogl (aktp column, from 2003)
- Loan distribution stats (number of borrowers, median debt, percentiles): dnmuf1 (all sectors + instruments), dnmuf2 (realkreditlån + banklån, by interest type and afdrag), dnmuvs (erhverv by company size). All from 2020.
- Financial soundness ratios (capital ratios, NPL, liquidity): dnfsi (from 2005)
- Long loan series for husholdninger and non-financial firms back to 2003: dnmudl (PI+RI separate, nominal value)
- Danmarks Nationalbank foreign reserves: dnra (from 2015, mio. kr.) or dnintval (from 1999, mia. kr.)
- Nationalbanken's monetary policy instruments (folio, indskudsbeviser, pengepolitiske udlån): dnprnd (daily from 2005)
- Nationalbanken's balance sheet: dnsnb1 (holdings, from 1987) or dnsnb2 (transactions, from 1987)
- Currency intervention by Nationalbanken: dnvali (daily net FX purchases, 1999-2022)
- Credit conditions survey: dnudpriv (private borrowers), dnuderhv (corporate borrowers), both quarterly from 2008, values are net balance statistics

## Common gotchas applying to most tables
- `data='ULT'` (or `data='ULTIMO'`/`data='ULTIMONOM'`) selects end-of-period stock. Other data values are flow adjustments (NET, VAL, KI=index). Always filter data.
- Sector hierarchies: codes named '1000', '1000.0', '10XXDK', or similar are totals. Sub-sector codes (1100, 1200, 1300, 1400...) are components. Never sum sub-sectors with their parent.
- instrnat: AL00/PL00 = loan/deposit total. Other codes are sub-types. Don't sum sub-types with the total.
- valuta: Z01=alle valutaer (typical total), X00=foreign currencies. Neither joins dim.valuta_iso — treat as inline aggregate codes.
- branche in DNP/DNR tables uses NACE section ZZ-codes (AZZ, BZZ, CZZ...) not numeric DB07 — the dim.db link is incorrect for these tables.
- sektornat in some MFI tables (dnmnogl, dnmin) uses compound sector+country codes (e.g. '1100DK') that do not join dim.esr_sekt.
- Unit: most DNP/DNR/DNM tables use mio. kr.; Nationalbanken tables (dnintval, dnbetal, dnprnd, dnsnb1, dnsnb2, dnvali) use mia. kr. Don't mix.
