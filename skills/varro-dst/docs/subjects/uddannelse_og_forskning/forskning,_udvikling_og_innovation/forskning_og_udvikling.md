notes:
- The subject splits into three groups by coverage: (1) erhvervsliv (forsk* tables), (2) offentlig sektor (fouoff* tables), (3) cross-sector totals and budgets (rdce*, foubud*, cfabnp).

## Erhvervsliv (private sector R&D) — forsk* tables
- forsk01: Total own R&D spending by industry/size/region + cost breakdown (drift/investering). The main reference table for business R&D. From 2007.
- forsk02: Purchased R&D spending by supplier type (domestic/foreign). Complements forsk01. From 2007.
- forsk09: Own R&D operating costs by research type (grundforskning/anvendt/udviklingsarbejde). Operating costs only, no capital. From 2013.
- forsk110/forsk111: Financing sources for own (110) vs. purchased (111) R&D. From 2013.
- forsk112: Own R&D spending by scientific field (vidomr). Shortest series, from 2017 only.
- forsk113/forsk114: R&D personnel headcount (113) and FTE (114) by staff type and gender. From 2013.
- ALL forsk* tables share the same fui01 multi-perspective column — see forsk01 notes. The key rule: fui01 encodes industry, technology level, IT-branches, knowledge services, size groups, and regions in one column. Pick one perspective per query.

## Offentlig sektor — fouoff* tables
- fouoff01/fouoff02: Public-sector R&D personnel headcount (01) and FTE (02) by sub-sector, field, staff category, and gender. From 2007. Identical structure.
- fouoff03/fouoff04: R&D FTE by research purpose — absolute (03) and percentage (04). The percentage table (fouoff04) has pre-computed shares summing to 100%; never re-sum those.
- fouoff06: University-level FTE breakdown by individual institution and academic rank. Only covers 8 universities under universitetsloven.
- fouoff07: UNUSUAL — drift1 column mixes units (årsværk when drift1='0', mio. kr. for drift1 200/210/220). Always filter drift1 before using.
- fouoff09: Externally financed R&D costs by sector, field, and funding source. From 2007.

## Cross-sector & budgets — rdce*, foubud*, cfabnp
- rdce01: Total R&D spending by sector and financing source. The broadest cross-sector spending table. From 2008.
- rdce02: Foreign financing of R&D by sector and source. Drill-down into rdce01's "Udlandet" category. From 2007.
- rdce03: R&D spending by sector and cost type (drift/anlæg). From 2009.
- rdce042: R&D spending by sector and research type (grundforskning/anvendt/eksperimentel). All sectors. From 2009.
- rdce05: R&D spending by sector and scientific field. From 2008.
- rdce06: Government R&D spending by socio-economic purpose (health, defense, agriculture, etc.). From 2008.
- rdcp0129: All-sector R&D personnel with FTE/headcount selector (aarvaerk). Cross-sector overview. From 2008.
- foubud: Public research budget (finanslov + internationale + kommunale + fonde) by source. Best for long-run budget trends (2001–2025). Contains mixed mio.kr./pct codes — filter bevilling carefully.
- foubud1: Most detailed budget table: research purpose × sector × funding type. From 2007.
- foubud4: Budget by sector × funding type only. Has future projections to 2028. Simplest sector budget table.
- foubud5: Budget by research purpose × funding type. Future projections to 2028.
- cfabnp: R&D intensity (% of BNP) for total, business, and public sector. Longest series (1997–2023). Use for R&D-to-GDP benchmarking.

## Common pitfalls
- foubud.prisenhed is a selector that doubles all rows (AARPRIS vs 08PRIS). Always filter to one.
- foubud1/foubud4/foubud5: bevilling1 is a selector (I alt / Basis / Program). Always filter to one.
- rdce* and rdcp0129 all have sektor='10' as the grand total — include it or exclude it deliberately.
- fouoff07 drift1 mixes units — see fouoff07 notes.
- rdcp0129 aarvaerk mixes FTE and headcount in one column — always filter aarvaerk to one value.
- Map: All forsk* tables support region-level choropleth maps via /geo/regioner.parquet. Filter fui01 IN (81,82,83,84,85) for the region perspective and merge on fui01=dim_kode. fouoff*, rdce*, foubud*, cfabnp, and rdcp0129 have no geographic breakdown.
