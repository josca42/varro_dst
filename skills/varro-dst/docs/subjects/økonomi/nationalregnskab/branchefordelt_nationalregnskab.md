notes:
- **V-prefix join is universal**: Every branche/erhverv code in all these tables has a "V" prefix (e.g. VA, VB, VD_E). The dim.nr_branche codes have no prefix. Always strip the V: `substring(f.branche from 2) = d.kode`. For niveau-1 tables (10-grouping), also convert underscores to hyphens: `replace(substring(f.branche from 2), '_', '-') = d.kode`. Direct join gives 0% match.
- **Three families**: NABB* (employment and hours), NABP* (production, GVA, income), NABK* (capital stock and investment). Number suffix = industry granularity: 10=niveau1 (13 sectors), 19=niveau2 (21), 36=niveau3 (38), 69=niveau4 (69), 117=niveau5 (117, only to 2022). Prefix NKB* = quarterly seasonal variant (1990–present); NAB* = annual (1966–present).
- **Which table for which question**:
  - Employment and hours by sector: NABB*. nabb10 for 13 broad sectors, nabb36/nabb69 for more detail. socio selects the measure (hours vs persons; all employed vs employees only).
  - GVA/production/income by sector: NABP*. transakt=B1GD gives GVA; filter prisenhed to V (current) or LAN (real). nabpdiv = nabp10 + extra TXB/TXMEMO adjustment rows for full reconciliation.
  - Capital stock and investment by sector: NABK*. behold=P51G gives gross investment; behold=LSN/LEN gives net capital stock.
  - Quarterly data: nkbb10 (employment), nkbp10 (production). Note prisenhed code for volume is LKV in quarterly tables, not LAN.
  - Revision vintages: vnabb10 and vnabp10 hold all historical releases.
- **Selector columns — always filter all of them** (each is repeated for every branche×tid, so omitting one silently multiplies counts):
  - socio (NABB*): 4 measurement types — always pick one.
  - transakt (NABP*): accounting aggregates that are identities of each other, never sum — pick one (B1GD for GVA).
  - behold (NABK*): stocks and flows mixed together, never sum — pick one.
  - prisenhed (NABP*, NABK*): V=current prices, LAN/LKV=2020 chain-linked — always filter.
  - saeson (NKB* quarterly): N=unadjusted, Y=seasonally adjusted — always filter.
- **VMEMO** appears in all tables as an unlabeled memo aggregate (not in dim, much smaller than V total). Exclude from standard aggregations along with V.
