notes:
## Two accounting principles — pick the right one
- **Economy principle** (grønt nationalregnskab): attributes emissions to Danish economic units, including Danish ships/planes operating abroad. Tables: drivhus, drivhus2, mru1, ene3h, ene2ho/ht/ha, ene1ho/ht/ha.
- **Territorial principle** (UNFCCC): emissions physically occurring in Denmark. Bridge between the two is in mro1 (all pollutants) and mro2 (GHGs in CO2-equivalents).
- The tema9007 overview table shows both totals side by side.

## Greenhouse gas tables (CO2-equivalents)
- **drivhus**: GHG by sector (1.000 ton, 1990–2024). Best for sector analysis. Joins dim.nr_branche with V-prefix + underscore→hyphen transform.
- **drivhus2**: Same as drivhus but adds opprincip (direct vs. allocated el/fjernvarme). Use when you need Scope 1 vs. Scope 2 style splits. Always filter opprincip.
- **mro2**: Bridge table (national accounts ↔ UNFCCC GHGs in CO2-equivalents). Use to reconcile the two principles.
- **tema9007**: Quick headline totals (Mio ton). Two series: economy vs. territorial.
- **tema9005**: Broad 4-sector breakdown (agriculture, industry, utilities, other) in 1.000 ton.

## Broad air emissions (all pollutants including CO2, SO2, NOx, PM)
- **mru1**: Full air emissions by sector and emtype8 (15 pollutants). Units differ by emtype8 — filter to one. Use when you need non-GHG pollutants.
- **mro1**: Bridge table for all pollutants (same structure as mro2).

## Energy tables
- **ene3h**: Gross energy consumption by sector and fuel type in GJ (1966–2024). Best for sector energy use.
- **ene2ho**: Energy balance supply/use overview in GJ (1966–2024). Use for total supply, imports, exports.
- **ene2ht / ene2ha**: Detailed supply (tilgang) and use (anvend) sides in GJ. Use when you need sector-level production or consumption detail.
- **ene1ho / ene1ht / ene1ha**: Same structure as ene2* but in native units (ton, TJ, GWh per fuel type). Do NOT sum across fuel types — units are incompatible. Use when you need physical quantities by fuel.
- **sdg07021**: Single-series renewable share in % for Denmark (1990–2024). No sector detail.

## Multiplier/footprint tables (input-output based)
- **ene2mu1n**: Energy footprint by sector (direct + indirect), 3 mult types — always filter mult and prisenhed.
- **ene2mu2n**: Energy footprint by final demand category (household consumption, investment, exports).
- **ene2mu3n**: Energy by sector attributed to final demand origin. Ends at 2022.
- **emm1mu1n**: Emissions footprint by sector (15 pollutants), same structure as ene2mu1n.
- **emm1mu2n**: Emissions footprint by final demand category.
- **emm1mu3n**: Emissions by sector attributed to final demand. Ends at 2023.
- **aftryk1**: Climate footprint (experimental) — total Danish CO2 footprint incl. imported emissions by source country and demand category. Very different concept from drivhus.

## Municipal/regional overview tables (short series)
- **laby33**: Energy indicators by kommunegruppe (2020–2022). Filter enhed to avoid overcounting.
- **laby34**: GHG indicators by kommunegruppe (2020–2022). Filter enhed and emtype8.

## International comparisons (limited coverage)
- **tema9003**: Renewable share by country (2019–2021 only).
- **tema9008**: GHG per capita by country (2000–2020).

## Thematic overview tables (Denmark only)
- **tema9001**: GHG + GDP index series (1990=100). For decoupling analysis only — values are indices, not absolute.
- **tema9006**: Household CO2 by energy source (transport fuel, heating, el/fjernvarme indirect).
- **tema9010**: Agriculture/forestry/fishing GHG by type (CO2, N2O, CH4) in Mio ton CO2-eq.
- **tema9012**: GHG intensity (ton per mio. kr. value added) for all industries aggregate.

## Common pitfalls across this subject
- **V-prefix branche join**: Tables with V-prefixed NACE codes (drivhus, drivhus2, mru1, ene3h, ene2mu1n, emm1mu1n, emm1mu3n, aftryk1.udledbranche) need: REPLACE(REPLACE(col, 'V', ''), '_', '-') = TRIM(d.kode) to join dim.nr_branche.
- **mult and prisenhed selectors**: The mu1n/mu2n tables multiply row counts 3× (mult) × 2× (prisenhed). Always filter both columns before aggregating.
- **emtype8 mixed units**: In mru1 and emm* tables, units differ per emtype8 code. Never sum across emtype8 values.
- **Hierarchy levels**: branche columns span NACE levels 1–5 simultaneously. Filter by dim.nr_branche.niveau or use the aggregate codes (MTOT/ETOT/EV) to avoid double-counting.
