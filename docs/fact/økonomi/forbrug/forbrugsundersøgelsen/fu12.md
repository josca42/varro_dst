table: fact.fu12
description: Husstandenes gennemsnitlige forbrug efter forbrugsgruppe, prisenhed og tid
measure: indhold (unit Kr. pr. husstand)
columns:
- konsumgrp: join dim.ecoicop on konsumgrp=kode::text [approx]; levels [1, 2]
- prisenhed: values [AARPRIS=Løbende priser, 08PRIS=Faste priser]
- tid: date range 2015-01-01 to 2023-01-01
dim docs: /dim/ecoicop.md

notes:
- prisenhed MUST be filtered — it exactly doubles all rows. Use AARPRIS (current prices) or 08PRIS (constant 2008 prices). Forgetting this silently doubles every sum.
- konsumgrp uses ECOICOP dotted decimal notation (e.g. "00", "01", "01.1.1.1"), NOT the integer kode in dim.ecoicop. Standard join `f.konsumgrp = d.kode::text` won't work. Use: `REPLACE(f.konsumgrp, '.', '')::integer = d.kode AND d.niveau = (LENGTH(f.konsumgrp) - LENGTH(REPLACE(f.konsumgrp, '.', ''))) + 1`
- fu12 has 2 hierarchy levels in konsumgrp: 14 niveau-1 codes ("00"=total, "01"-"13") and 267 niveau-4 codes ("01.1.1.1" etc.). No niveau 2 or 3 here — use fu13-fu18 for that.
- 125 of 281 konsumgrp codes don't match dim.ecoicop: DST-specific subcodes ending in ".0" or ".9" (e.g. "01.2.1.0", "09.1.2.9") and category "13.x" (forsikring/finansielle tjenester) which is a DST addition beyond standard ECOICOP.
- konsumgrp "00" = total consumption (all categories), maps to no dim code.
